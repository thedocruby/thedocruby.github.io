Hello! I'm Dr. Rubisco  (DOCK-tore Roo-BISS-coe), but my friends call me Doc. I am a passionate 3D Tech Artist and Immersive Effects Engineer, with a talent for the clever use of math, science, and logic in my code. I take pride in my work, and strive to produce the best product possible. I am passionate and skilled with the scientific and mathematical aspects of programming, with emphasis on procedural, parametric, modular, and iterative applications. My strongest skills include aspects of game dev such as graphics, audio, physics, and procedural generation/animation , as well as optimization and debugging.  

Have a look at some of my work!

Iris Shaders is the world-famous custom shading engine for the Minecraft Fabric mod loader. By taking advantage of compatibilities with mods such as CaffeineMC's Sodium, Iris achieves record breaking framerates, on top of custom visual effects that give the game a wonderful sense of character and quality. This was my first project as a Technical Artist, contributing to the development of an efficient and versatile shader pipeline while learning graphics programming along the way. Today, I'm proud to say that Iris Shaders is one of the most popular mods in the entire MC modding community

Resounding is an immersive audio effects engine for Minecraft that uses optimized ​​ray tracing to calculate realistic and immersive dynamic effects that change with the sandbox environment. This is a rewrite of an earlier project of mine, and was the first game development project that I spearheadded myself. Since the Minecraft world is not predetermined at compile time, common immersive audio techniques do not work. Creating a dynamic effect system for an open world sandbox game like Minecraft presented a set of unique problems that challenged my skills and helped me grow as a developer.

Catch '22 is my most recent project; a VR game made with Unity and UE5; a fun an exciting version of the classic game of catch, updated for the modern world of 2022. I learned about all aspects of game development for this project, from concept to publishing. I also gained some valuable firsthand experience with VR development, and I learned new skills and techniques that will be useful for many years to come. I don't yet have a website up for this project, but one is in the works. I also plan to publish the Unity version to the Oculus Store, after i polish it up a bit. In the meantime, you can contact me for more info.

3D Art and Graphic Design are also areas where I find a strong interest. My work in the art and design field compliments my game dev experience, and helps me more thoroughly understand the work of an artist, so I can effectively bridge the gap between tech and art, and contribute in both areas as needed.

I have Skills & Experience in both technical and creative applications. I can use this experience to smoothly integrate the code and artwork in a way that makes entire games look like one coherent work of art.

My experience with game engtines and 3d modeling software help me understand artistry from the artist's perspective, and allow me contribute there as needed. This also goes for my Adobe Creative Cloud experience: software like Photoshop and Illustrator are often overlooked, but are an essential step in many artistic pipelines.

I have experience with a wide variety of programming languages, including Python, C#, C++, Java, SQL, and more. My flexibility in this area allows me to pick up a project, new or old, and start producing useful results as quickly as possible.

Through my endless wandering through different tech and art fields, I have also gained a general understanding of how the system works, which allows me to learn new concepts and techniques in a flash, and start applying them rigtht away.
